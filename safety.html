Flood Safety Measures


Stay informed about weather forecasts and flood warnings. Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, medications, and first aid supplies. Have a family communication plan in place. Know evacuation routes and have a plan for where to go if evacuated. Elevate electrical appliances and utilities if possible to minimize damage. Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters. If evacuation is necessary, follow instructions from local authorities and evacuate promptly.

Preventing Illness:


Avoid contact with floodwaters as they may contain contaminants and pathogens. Use protective gear like boots and gloves if you must enter floodwaters. Wash hands frequently with soap and clean water, especially before eating or preparing food. Avoid consuming food or water that may have been contaminated by floodwaters. If using a private well for drinking water, have it tested for contamination before consuming. Seek medical attention if you develop symptoms like fever, diarrhea, or vomiting after exposure to floodwaters. Be cautious of potential mold growth in flooded areas and take steps to clean and dry affected areas promptly to prevent respiratory issues.
